About the AGM Center

Bridging research, policy, and practice to advance Korea's power system transformation through open-source tools, rigorous analysis, and collaborative engagement

Who We Are

Founded on May 20, 2025, the Advanced Grid Modeling (AGM) Research Centre addresses technical and market barriers through advanced modeling, stakeholder dialogue, and professional education

Our Mission

Empowering Korea’s energy transition through open-source grid modelling and collaborative stakeholder engagement

The Advanced Grid Modeling (AGM) Center was established to address the growing complexity of Korea’s power system transformation. As renewable energy penetration increases and market structures evolve, decision-makers need reliable, transparent, and accessible analytical tools.

We believe that open-source tools, rigorous research, and inclusive stakeholder engagement are essential to navigating this transition successfully.

What We Do

Three Pillars Supporting Korea's Energy Transition

The AGM Research Centre addresses technical and market barriers through our integrated approach

Advanced & Reproducible Modelling

Mathematical representation of grid physics and market structure with DERs. Modelling VRE uncertainty with probabilistic methods. Enabling reproducibility through open data and transparent methodology.

Open Discussion between Stakeholders

A hub for fostering dialogue and collaboration among diverse stakeholders. Facilitating shared understanding and meaningful exchanges between the system operator (KPX) and the sole power utility (KEPCO).
