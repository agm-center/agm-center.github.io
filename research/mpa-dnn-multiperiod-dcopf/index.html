MPA-DNN: Projection-Aware Unsupervised Learning for Multi-Period DC-OPF

Optimal Power Flow Multi-period Dispatch Projection-aware Learning Differentiable Optimization Energy Storage

A constraint-guaranteed deep learning framework for multi-period OPF that enforces feasibility through differentiable projection, enabling label-free training and physically valid dispatch trajectories.

By AGM Center Research Team

Overview

Deep neural networks are becoming powerful surrogates for Optimal Power Flow (OPF), offering near-instant solutions that can support real-time grid operation.
But existing learning-based OPF approaches still face three fundamental barriers:

  1. Feasibility — NN outputs often violate physical constraints
  2. Label dependence — Supervised learning requires expensive solver-generated labels
  3. Inter-temporal coupling — Ramping, storage SoC, and multi-period consistency are rarely enforced

To address these issues, this paper proposes a Multi-Period Projection-Aware Deep Neural Network (MPA-DNN).

MPA-DNN integrates a differentiable projection layer that enforces all power system constraints over a 24-hour horizon—including generator ramp limits and ESS dynamics.
The model learns in a fully unsupervised manner by minimizing generation cost, requiring no ground-truth OPF labels.

The result:

  • Feasible in every forward pass
  • Near-optimal cost
  • Zero ramping violations across all test conditions
  • Superior generalization to unseen load patterns

Motivation

1. OPF is harder than ever

Modern power systems include:

  • High renewable variability
  • Energy storage participation
  • Multi-period operational dependencies

These factors make OPF increasingly high-dimensional and computationally demanding.

2. Neural networks are fast… but unsafe

Existing learning-based OPF models struggle with:

  • Violating generator limits & power balance
  • Producing infeasible multi-period schedules
  • Failing under out-of-distribution load profiles

3. Supervised learning is impractical

A learning model for OPF typically needs:

  • Thousands of solver-generated labels
  • Multi-period OPF solutions (extremely expensive)
  • Scenario coverage across many load conditions

Label generation quickly becomes the bottleneck.

4. Inter-temporal constraints are critical

Ignoring generator ramping or ESS SoC creates schedules that are:

  • Physically impossible
  • Operationally unsafe
  • Misleadingly low-cost

Real-world operators need feasible trajectories, not just pointwise predictions.

Methodology

Fig. 1 Overall framework

The MPA-DNN architecture consists of:

  1. A Deep Neural Network

    • Inputs: 24-hour load profile
    • Outputs: unconstrained dispatch vector ( z )

  2. A Differentiable Projection Layer

    • Solves a quadratic program to project ( z ) onto the feasible set ( \mathcal{F} ) defined by all multi-period DC-OPF constraints
    • Enforces:
      • Power balance
      • Generator limits
      • Ramping limits
      • ESS energy dynamics
      • Line power flow constraints

  3. Unsupervised Loss Function
    [ L = \sum_{t=1}^{T} c_t^\top p_{g,t}^{e} ] No labels required—training directly minimizes system cost using feasible dispatch.

Differentiable Projection Layer

Given unconstrained NN output ( z ), the projection solves:

[ x^e = \arg\min_{x \in \mathcal{F}} \frac{1}{2} | x - z |_2^2 ]

This is a constrained QP ensuring strict feasibility at all times.

The KKT system allows full backpropagation through the projection layer.

Multi-period DC-OPF Constraints Modeled

Constraints include:

  • Generator dispatch limits
  • ESS SoC dynamics & bounds
  • Ramp-up/down constraints
  • Line flow limits via GSF matrix
  • Initial and terminal SoC conditions

Results

1. Generalization Under Distribution Shift

Models trained at nominal load scale (1.000) are tested at +2.5% and +5% higher load.
MPA-DNN maintains the best accuracy and lowest optimality gap across all load scales.

3. Economic Performance

Results show that:

  • SPA-DNN gives slightly lower cost at hour 16 but violates ramp limits → infeasible, unusable
  • MPA-DNN matches the solver cost almost exactly at all hours

Thus, the model achieves near-optimal cost without violating physics.

Impact

✔ Fully unsupervised multi-period OPF learning

No need for costly solver-generated labels.

✔ Strict feasibility at every training & inference step

Thanks to the embedded projection layer.

✔ Handles generator ramping & ESS dynamics

A capability missing in most OPF learning frameworks.

✔ Strong generalization under distribution shift

Outperforms supervised baselines even in unseen conditions.

✔ Practical for real-time OPF applications

Fast forward inference after training, with guaranteed constraint satisfaction.

Future Work

  • Extend projection-aware learning to security-constrained AC-OPF
  • Incorporate unit commitment decisions
  • Model multi-ESS coordination and flexible load
  • Provide computational benchmarking for real-time control rooms
  • Explore hybrid differentiable optimization + reinforcement learning for sequential decision-making
