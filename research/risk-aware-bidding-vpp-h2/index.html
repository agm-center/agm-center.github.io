Risk-Aware Bi-Level Bidding Strategy for Virtual Power Plants with Power-to-Hydrogen Integration

Virtual Power Plant Power-to-Hydrogen Bi-level Optimization Risk Management Electricity Markets

Bi-level bidding with CVaR-based risk mitigation and hydrogen storage enables VPPs to enhance revenue, hedge financial risks, and improve flexibility under volatile market conditions.

By AGM Center Research Team

Overview

Virtual Power Plants (VPPs) play an increasingly important role in aggregating distributed energy resources such as PV, wind, energy storage systems (ESS), and emerging hydrogen-based assets.
However, volatile electricity markets and renewable uncertainty expose VPP operators to substantial financial risks.

This research proposes a risk-aware bi-level bidding strategy that integrates a Power-to-Hydrogen (P2H) system and applies Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) to balance revenue maximization and risk mitigation.
The approach enables VPPs to perform strategic bidding while ensuring market-clearing feasibility.

The model is validated using a modified IEEE 14-bus system, demonstrating that hydrogen storage significantly enhances both profitability and robustness compared to ESS-only or renewable-only portfolios.

Research Motivation

Modern VPPs face two primary operational challenges:

1. Renewable Variability

PV and wind generation fluctuate significantly, complicating scheduling and profitable bidding.

2. Market Volatility

Electricity prices are increasingly unstable, leading to large downside financial risks.

While ESS helps mitigate some uncertainty, hydrogen storage systems (HSS)—via electrolyzers, hydrogen tanks, and fuel cells—offer new opportunities:

  • Greater flexibility than ESS
  • Ability to charge and discharge simultaneously
  • Expected lower Levelized Cost of Storage (LCOS) by 2030 compared to Li-ion batteries

Despite this potential, few existing studies incorporate hydrogen systems into bi-level bidding optimization under uncertainty.

Methodology

Fig. 1 Framework Diagram.

Bi-Level Optimization Framework

The proposed formulation consists of:

Upper Level — VPP Bidding Strategy

  • Maximize expected revenue
  • Incorporate CVaR-based risk aversion
  • Optimize operation of PV, WT, ESS, and HSS
  • Determine strategic bid quantity

The upper-level decision variables include:

  • ESS charging/discharging
  • Electrolyzer and fuel cell operation
  • Hydrogen tank storage dynamics
  • CVaR variables (VaR and auxiliary slack variables)

Lower Level — Market Clearing Model

  • DC power flow
  • Generator limits
  • Line capacity constraints
  • Locational Marginal Price (LMP) determination

The lower-level problem is linear and is converted into an MPEC using KKT conditions.

Key Innovations

1. CVaR-Based Risk-Aware Bidding

The model ensures VPPs:

  • Avoid extreme financial losses
  • Adapt bidding behavior to market conditions
  • Trade off expected revenue vs. downside protection

2. Simultaneous Charging/Discharging in HSS

From Fig. 3(c) and 3(d) in the paper:

  • Electrolyzer produces hydrogen
  • Fuel cell converts hydrogen to electricity
  • Both can operate concurrently (unlike ESS)

This unique behavior improves:

  • Arbitrage opportunities
  • Operational flexibility
  • Revenue under price uncertainty

3. MPEC Reformulation for Tractable Optimization

Using the KKT conditions allows:

  • Efficient computation
  • Guaranteed optimality in the lower level
  • Integration of market-clearing logic into the upper decision-making process

Case Study

Test System

  • Modified IEEE 14-bus network
  • Five renewable generation scenarios
  • Scenario probabilities: {0.3, 0.25, 0.2, 0.15, 0.1}

Results

1. Profitability Comparison Across Resource Portfolios

CaseDER PortfolioExpected Revenue ($)
(1)PV + WT3,710
(2)PV + WT + ESS4,405 (↑18.7%)
(3)PV + WT + HSS4,607 (↑24.2%)

Hydrogen storage yields the highest revenue increase, surpassing ESS due to:

  • Round-trip flexibility
  • Enhanced arbitrage opportunities
  • Ability to operate fuel cell and electrolyzer simultaneously

2. Effect of Hydrogen Tank Size

  • Larger hydrogen tanks support greater charging–discharging flexibility
  • However, diminishing returns must be considered when installation cost is included

3. Impact of Risk Aversion (β Parameter)

As β increases:

  • Expected revenue decreases (risk-averse strategy)
  • Larger HSS tanks show lower sensitivity to risk
  • Small tanks or ESS-only systems lose revenue more sharply

Figure 4 in the paper highlights:

  • Double-size HSS maintains revenue stability
  • Original or half-sized tanks degrade more noticeably under risk

Scenario-wise results (Fig. 5) show:

  • High renewable scenarios → revenue decreases under risk aversion
  • Low renewable scenarios → revenue increases due to conservative bidding

This means:

Risk aversion helps avoid losses during low generation conditions while damping aggressive bidding in high-renewable scenarios.

Insights & Contributions

✔ Hydrogen storage significantly improves VPP profitability

Compared to ESS, HSS offers superior arbitrage and flexibility.

✔ Risk-aware bidding stabilizes revenue under uncertainty

CVaR protects against tail-risk events and adapts strategy to market volatility.

✔ Bi-level MPEC formulation enables realistic market participation

The approach respects market-clearing conditions and LMP formation.

✔ Simultaneous operation of electrolyzer and fuel cell is a game changer

This enables round-trip flexibility impossible for traditional storage systems.

Future Directions

The paper identifies several extensions:

  1. Real-time bidding and multi-market participation
  2. Coupled electricity–hydrogen markets
  3. Multi-period risk management with dynamic CVaR
  4. Co-optimization with transmission constraints and congestion forecasting

Conclusion

This study demonstrates that a CVaR-based bi-level bidding strategy combined with Power-to-Hydrogen systems can significantly enhance both profitability and risk robustness of VPPs.
Hydrogen storage not only increases revenue but also reduces exposure to market volatility, making it a promising asset for future grid-integrated VPP operations.

Reference

Yoo, J., & Kim, J. “A Risk-aware Bi-level Bidding Strategy for Virtual Power Plant with Power-to-Hydrogen System.” 2025 IEEE Power & Energy Society General Meeting (PESGM)
