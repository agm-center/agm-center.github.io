KPG 193: A Synthetic Korean Power Grid Test System for Decarbonization Research

Synthetic Grid Modeling Korean Power System Decarbonization Renewable Integration Open Data

An open, 193-bus synthetic model of the Korean transmission system—designed for decarbonization, renewable integration, and expansion planning studies using real 2022 demand, weather, and renewable datasets.

By AGM Center Research Team

Overview

High-quality open-source power grid test systems are essential for studying decarbonization pathways, renewable integration, climate impacts, and grid planning.
However, unlike Europe and the United States, Korea has no publicly available transmission-level test system that reflects its unique grid characteristics:

  • Low renewable penetration
  • Heavy dependence on coal and LNG
  • Highly concentrated metropolitan demand
  • Generation sited along the east/south coasts
  • No synchronous interconnection with neighboring countries

To address this gap, this work introduces KPG 193, a synthetic Korean power grid test system built from open data sources.
It includes:

  • 193 buses
  • 122 generators
  • 407 transmission lines
  • Hourly 2022 demand + LDAPS weather + renewable profiles

The system is validated using full-year ACOPF and Unit Commitment simulations and is fully released for the research community.

Motivation

1. Korea lacks an open, high-fidelity test system

Most Korean grid data are not publicly disclosed. Existing public models are:

  • A coarse 11-zone system (useful but oversimplified)
  • A 1,428-bus realistic model (not open to the public)

This creates a barrier for academic and industrial decarbonization research.

2. Synthetic grids enable reproducible decarbonization studies

Europe and the US have SciGRID, osmTGmod, PyPSA-Eur, and Texas backbone systems—driving massive research progress.
Korea needs equivalent open models for:

  • Renewable integration
  • Energy storage placement
  • Transmission expansion
  • High-resolution climate-driven studies

3. Korea faces unique operational challenges

KPG 193 is designed to reflect real system characteristics:

  • 40% of national load concentrated in Seoul metropolitan area
  • Generation heavily located on the eastern coastline
  • Solar and wind resources concentrated in the southwest
  • Long transmission distances → voltage issues + congestion

Methodology

Fig. 1 Network topology and generation mix for the KPG 193 test system

The modeling workflow combines open datasets at different spatial resolutions into a unified grid representation.

Step 1 — Open Data Collection

Network topology

  • Overhead/underground line geometries from OSM
  • Voltage levels and conductor attributes inferred from tags
  • AC parameters computed using manufacturer catalogs
  • HVDC line (Bukdangjin–Godeok, 1,500 MVA) manually added

Generation data

  • KPX locations and capacities for coal, LNG, nuclear, hydro
  • Renewable (solar/wind) installed capacity from Korea Energy Agency

Weather data

  • LDAPS: 1.5 km resolution hourly solar, temperature, wind (2022)

Demand data

  • KPX: hourly national demand
  • KEPCO: municipal-level monthly consumption (230 regions)

Population + utility regional offices

  • KEPCO’s 193 service regions → used as clustering anchors

Clustering-Based System Construction

Because detailed topology is not publicly available, the authors apply spatial clustering using 193 KEPCO regional offices, treating each office as a bus in the synthetic grid.

Mapping procedure

  • Power plants → assigned to nearest bus
  • OSM line endpoints → snapped to nearest bus, with manual adjustments
  • Municipal demand → allocated to buses based on population ratios
  • Renewable profiles → computed per bus using LDAPS weather
  • Each bus receives:
    • Hourly load
    • Solar PV potential
    • Wind speed profile
    • Temperature signal

This ensures both tractability and realistic spatial distribution.

Renewable profiles

Wind and solar output are computed using standard turbine and PV models:

  • Wind: cut-in, rated, cut-out speeds
  • Solar: fill factor + irradiance and temperature adjustments

System Description

The final KPG 193 network includes:

  • Voltage levels: 154 kV, 345 kV, 765 kV AC + 500 kV HVDC
  • Buses: 193
  • Lines: 407
  • Generators: 122 (coal, LNG, nuclear, hydro, solar, wind)
  • Renewables: Represented with realistic weather-driven temporal profiles

Validation

Validation uses:

  • Daily Unit Commitment (UC)
  • Hourly ACOPF for all 8,760 hours in 2022

Generation cost coefficients were adapted from NREL datasets and modified.

1. Annual UC & ACOPF Simulation

  • All UC problems converged
  • All ACOPF problems feasible
  • Annual generation deviations within 1.4% of actual KPX 2022 data

2. Snapshot Validation — July 22, 2022 (Summer Peak)

Power flow patterns

  • Metropolitan area (circled) → ~40% of national load
  • East coast → dominated by coal & nuclear → large exports
  • Result: Strong northward flows on 345/765 kV and HVDC lines
    → Matches real Korean operational patterns

Voltage patterns

  • Southwestern renewable-rich zone → overvoltage tendency (light colors)
  • Eastern coastline → dense generation + long-distance transfer → voltage stress

These match known real-world characteristics of the Korean grid.

Limitations

The authors note several inherent limitations due to open-data constraints:

  • No modeling of reactive power control devices (SVC, STATCOM, tap changers)
  • OSM topology may be incomplete or outdated
  • Reactive power demand fixed at 90% of real demand
  • HVDC and transformer details simplified

Nevertheless, the synthetic system provides strong validity for research use.

Impact

✔ First openly available Korean transmission test system

Enables Korea-focused OPF, UC, expansion planning, renewable siting, and climate impact studies.

✔ Captures essential geographic and operational characteristics

Metropolitan demand, coastal generation, renewable clustering—core features for decarbonization modeling.

✔ Reproducible and extensible

Includes open-source:

  • MATPOWER-formatted network
  • Weather & demand time series (2022)
  • Renewable profiles
  • Generator parameters
  • Maintenance schedules

✔ Foundation for Korean energy transition modeling

Useful for:

  • Dynamic line rating studies
  • OTS / UC / OPF algorithm development
  • Policy simulations for 2030/2050 carbon-neutral pathways
  • Stability and resiliency assessments

Future Work

The authors propose:

  • Adding detailed reactive power devices & transformer tap modeling
  • Incorporating multi-year weather datasets
  • Enhancing renewable modeling (e.g., offshore wind)
  • Developing stochastic and security-constrained versions
  • Validating against real power flow measurements when data becomes available
