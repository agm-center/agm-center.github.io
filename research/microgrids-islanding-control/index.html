Microgrid Control and Seamless Islanding for Enhanced Resilience

Microgrids Grid Resilience Control Systems

Advanced control strategies enabling microgrids to operate independently during main grid outages while maintaining power quality and enabling smooth reconnection.

By AGM Center Research Team

Introduction

Microgrids enhance power system resilience by enabling critical facilities to maintain operations during grid outages. This research develops advanced control algorithms for seamless grid-connected and islanded operation.

Control Architecture

Hierarchical Control Framework

Three-level control structure:

Primary Control (milliseconds):

  • Voltage and frequency regulation
  • Active and reactive power sharing
  • Grid-forming vs. grid-following modes

Secondary Control (seconds):

  • Voltage and frequency restoration
  • Economic optimization
  • State-of-charge balancing

Tertiary Control (minutes):

  • Energy management
  • Market participation
  • Grid synchronization

Seamless Islanding Detection

Our advanced detection algorithm:

  • Detects grid faults within 2 cycles (40 ms)
  • Distinguishes between local and system faults
  • Prevents unnecessary trips
  • Enables smooth transition to islanded mode

Results and Field Demonstrations

Deployed at 5 Korean facilities:

  • Zero customer interruptions during 12 grid disturbances
  • <0.5 Hz frequency deviation during islanding
  • <5% voltage deviation during transitions

Conclusion

Microgrid control technology is now mature enough for widespread deployment, offering significant resilience benefits for critical facilities and enabling higher renewable penetration in distribution systems.
