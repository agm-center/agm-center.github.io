KPG 193 Data Specifications

Detailed technical specifications for the KPG 193 v1.5 synthetic Korean power grid test system. For specific file formats and structures, see the dedicated documentation pages.

System Overview

The KPG 193 represents the Korean power grid as of 2022, capturing its characteristics as a high-coal, low-renewable isolated system suitable for decarbonization research.

Technology Characteristics

Coal-fired generation (46 units):

Unit sizes:

  • Large: 1000-1200 MW (8 units)
  • Medium: 500-1000 MW (28 units)
  • Small: 200-500 MW (10 units)

Operational parameters:

  • Minimum output: 40% of Pmax
  • Ramp rate: 10-13% Pmax per hour
  • Minimum up time: 6-12 hours
  • Minimum down time: 12 hours
  • Startup time: 6-10 hours (hot), 12-24 hours (cold)
  • Startup cost: 2,500-15,000 USD

Cost structure:

  • Variable cost: 22-32 USD/MWh
  • Heat rate: 9,500-10,500 Btu/kWh
  • Fuel: Coal-indexed pricing

Geographic: Coastal regions (cooling water access)

LNG (natural gas) generation (54 units):

Unit sizes:

  • Large CCGT: 880-990 MW (15 units)
  • Medium CCGT: 550-880 MW (20 units)
  • Small CCGT: 400-550 MW (14 units)
  • Peakers: 100-400 MW (5 units)

Operational parameters:

  • Minimum output: 38% of Pmax
  • Ramp rate: 40-45% Pmax per hour (flexible)
  • Minimum up time: 3-6 hours
  • Minimum down time: 3-6 hours
  • Startup time: 1-3 hours (hot), 3-6 hours (cold)
  • Startup cost: 500-3,000 USD

Cost structure:

  • Variable cost: 70-90 USD/MWh
  • Heat rate: 6,500-8,500 Btu/kWh (efficient CCGT)
  • Fuel: LNG spot + contract pricing

Geographic: Distributed, near load centers and LNG terminals

Nuclear generation (22 units):

Unit sizes:

  • Large APR-1400: 1400 MW (4 units)
  • Medium OPR-1000: 1000 MW (10 units)
  • Medium CANDU: 650-700 MW (4 units)
  • Older designs: 600-950 MW (4 units)

Operational parameters:

  • Minimum output: 95% of Pmax (inflexible)
  • Ramp rate: 30% Pmax per hour (rarely changed)
  • Minimum up time: 720 hours (30 days, continuous)
  • Minimum down time: 720-2160 hours (refueling)
  • Startup time: 72-120 hours (complex)
  • Startup cost: Very high (100,000+ USD)

Cost structure:

  • Variable cost: 8-12 USD/MWh (fuel only)
  • Fixed O&M: High (capital intensive)
  • Fuel: Uranium, long-term contracts

Geographic: Coastal sites (southeastern and eastern coasts)

Must-off periods: Planned refueling outages (30-90 days every 12-18 months)

Renewable Resources

Solar PV (~7,300 MW installed):

  • Southwestern regions: Highest concentration (best resource)
  • All buses: Some capacity (distributed installations)
  • Per-bus range: 10-187 MW

Performance characteristics:

  • Capacity factor: 13-15% annual average
  • Peak generation: Summer midday (80-95% of capacity)
  • Daily pattern: Zero night, ramp dawn-dusk, peak noon
  • Seasonal: Higher summer (longer days, better angles)

Technology mix: Utility-scale and distributed rooftop

Wind (~1,800 MW installed):

  • Jeju Island: Highest (offshore and onshore)
  • Coastal areas: Significant (good wind resource)
  • Interior: Limited (poor resource)

Performance characteristics:

  • Capacity factor: 20-25% annual average
  • Peak generation: Variable, often night/early morning
  • Daily pattern: Less predictable than solar
  • Seasonal: Higher winter (monsoon winds)

Technology mix: Mostly onshore turbines, some offshore

Small hydro (~400 MW installed):

  • Eastern mountains: Concentration (rivers)
  • Northern border: Some capacity
  • Flat western: Minimal

Performance characteristics:

  • Capacity factor: 30-40% annual average
  • Generation: Steady run-of-river base
  • Daily pattern: Relatively flat
  • Seasonal: Higher summer (monsoon rainfall)

Technology: Run-of-river dominant, limited reservoir storage

Line types:

  • Overhead lines: Majority of transmission
  • Underground cables: Urban areas, river crossings
  • HVDC: Jeju Island connection only

HVDC connection (Jeju Island):

  • Capacity: 1500 MVA bidirectional
  • Technology: Line-Commutated Converter (LCC)
  • Connection: Bus 183 (Godeok, mainland) ↔ Bus 190 (Bukdangjin, Jeju)
  • Purpose: Connect isolated island grid
  • Operation: Typically imports to Jeju, exports during high wind

Reference: GE Grid Solutions case study (2018)

Temporal patterns:

  • Night valley (00:00-06:00): 60-70% of peak
  • Morning ramp (06:00-09:00): +20-25% increase
  • Daytime (09:00-18:00): 85-95% of peak
  • Evening peak (18:00-21:00): 95-100% of peak
  • Night decline (21:00-24:00): -25-30% decrease

Seasonal variation:

  • Summer peak (July-Aug): 100% (air conditioning driven)
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): 90-95% (heating loads)
  • Spring/Fall (Mar-May, Sep-Nov): 70-85% (mild weather)

Geographic concentration:

  • Capital (Area 1): 40% of total load
  • Central (Area 2): 25%
  • Southern (Area 3): 20%
  • Eastern (Area 4): 12%
  • Jeju (Area 5): 3%

Reactive power modeling:

  • Fixed ratio: Q_demand = 0.1 × P_demand
  • Power factor: ~0.995 lagging
  • Constant: Does not vary with voltage

Reserve TypeRequirementPurpose
Spinning reserve5% of loadImmediate response (seconds)
Non-spinning reserve3% of loadFast start units (10 minutes)
Regulating reserve1% of loadFrequency regulation (continuous)
Total operating reserve~8-10% of loadCombined requirements

Implementation in UC:

  • Reserve constraints ensure sufficient online capacity
  • Nuclear and inflexible coal do not provide reserves
  • LNG units primary reserve providers

Limitations and Disclaimers

Users should be aware of the following limitations:

  1. Synthetic data: System is derived from open sources, not actual utility data
  2. Aggregation: 230 municipalities reduced to 193 buses
  3. Simplified topology: Some transmission details aggregated
  4. Single weather year: Only 2022 weather data available
  5. No dynamic devices: Reactive compensation, tap changers not modeled
  6. Fixed reactive loads: Q = 0.1 × P assumption
  7. Estimated line parameters: Based on standard values, not measured

Recommended use: System-level studies where exact local details not critical
