Renewable Capacity and Nuclear Must-Off

The KPG 193 test system includes detailed data on renewable energy resources and planned nuclear plant outages, essential for realistic unit commitment and economic dispatch studies.

The system separates renewable capacity data (how much is installed) from renewable generation profiles (how much is produced each hour):

  1. Renewable capacity files define the installed capacity at each bus
  2. Renewable profile files provide hourly capacity factors (0-1 ratio)
  3. Actual generation = Installed capacity × Capacity factor

Each renewable capacity file lists the installed capacity at each bus for one technology type.

Solar capacity (solar_generators_2022.csv):

Characteristics:

  • Total installed capacity: ~7,300 MW
  • Geographic distribution: Concentrated in southern and southwestern regions
  • Bus coverage: All 193 buses have some solar capacity
  • Capacity range: 10-187 MW per bus
  • Technology: Utility-scale and distributed solar PV

Spatial pattern:

  • Higher concentration in southern coastal regions (good solar resource)
  • Lower in northern and mountainous areas
  • Reflects actual 2022 installation patterns in Korea

Wind capacity (wind_generators_2022.csv):

Characteristics:

  • Total installed capacity: ~1,800 MW
  • Geographic distribution: Coastal areas and highlands
  • Bus coverage: Most buses (coastal and elevated regions)
  • Capacity range: 5-150 MW per bus
  • Technology: Onshore and offshore wind farms

Spatial pattern:

  • Concentrated on southwestern coast (Jeju Island region)
  • Eastern coastal areas (strong wind resource)
  • Mountain ridges in interior regions

Hydro capacity (hydro_generators_2022.csv):

Characteristics:

  • Total installed capacity: ~400 MW
  • Geographic distribution: Mountain regions with rivers
  • Bus coverage: Limited to areas with hydro resources
  • Capacity range: 2-50 MW per bus
  • Technology: Run-of-river dominant, some small reservoirs

Spatial pattern:

  • Concentrated in eastern mountainous regions
  • Northern border areas (major rivers)
  • Limited presence in flat western regions

Nuclear Must-Off Constraints

This file (nuclear_mustoff.csv) contains scheduled outages for nuclear power plants, representing planned maintenance periods and refueling shutdowns.

ColumnDescriptionUnitsRange

off_end_dayDay when outage endsdays1-365
off_end_timeHour when outage endshours1-24

Nuclear generator outages:

  • Affected units: 22 nuclear generators
  • Outage types: Planned maintenance and refueling
  • Duration: Typically 30-90 days per outage
  • Timing: Staggered to maintain grid reliability
  • Coverage: Year-round with peak maintenance in spring/fall

Why must-off constraints?

Nuclear plants in Korea operate in baseload mode but require periodic maintenance:

  1. Refueling outages: Every 12-18 months, lasting 30-60 days
  2. Planned maintenance: Additional downtime for inspections and repairs
  3. Regulatory requirements: Mandatory safety inspections
  4. Grid coordination: Scheduled to avoid peak demand periods

Research Applications

Renewable integration studies:

  1. Scenario analysis: Scale renewable capacity up/down
  2. Technology comparison: Solar vs. wind performance
  3. Siting studies: Evaluate new renewable locations
  4. Curtailment analysis: Identify overgeneration periods
  5. Storage integration: Size batteries to smooth renewable output

Nuclear flexibility studies:

  1. Maintenance scheduling: Optimize outage timing
  2. Reserve adequacy: Ensure sufficient backup capacity
  3. Flexibility requirements: Quantify ramping needs during outages
  4. Cost analysis: Economic impact of nuclear unavailability
  5. Reliability studies: Plan for forced and planned outages

Limitations

  • Renewable capacity: Represents 2022 installations; Korea’s renewable capacity has grown since
  • Spatial resolution: Capacity aggregated at bus level, not individual plant locations
  • Nuclear outages: Only planned outages included; forced outages not modeled
  • Hydro modeling: Simplified as run-of-river; reservoir operations not detailed
  • Offshore wind: Aggregated with onshore wind capacity
